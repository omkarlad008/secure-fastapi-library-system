IT Infrastructure and Security

Prof. Xiang

The IT industry is a rapidly changing one, needing constantly to adapt to new technology in order to make the best use of that technology. This adaptation typically happens within a framework which we call the IT infrastructure. This framework typically includes a wide diversity of hardware, networking and software solutions. In order to better understand how all these pieces fit together, and to better manage such connections, it is useful to look at the architecture of this framework - in other words, how can we best design or assemble the pieces, so that they work together optimally.

Database Concepts

Prof. Emily

Welcome to (Practical) Database Concepts! This course will be delivered fully online and by accessing materials and resources here in Canvas, with weekly lectorial and practical classes. Databases and database technology are having a major impact on the growing use of computers. They play a critical role in almost all areas where computers are used.

Programmming Fundamentals

Prof. Huang

This course will be delivered through face-to-face/online lectorials, practicals, and by accessing the online content here in Canvas. In this course, you will learn basic programming skills, and also put them into practice in writing simple software applications. Throughout the whole course, you will learn and practise all the programming concepts in Python, but you will also learn some basic knowledge of Java at the end of the course.

Data Science

Prof. Yongli

The course gives you a set of practical skills for handling data that comes in a variety of formats and sizes, such as numeric and text data. These skills cover the data analysis lifecycle from initial access and acquisition, modeling, transformation, integration, querying, application of statistical learning and data mining methods, and presentation of results.
